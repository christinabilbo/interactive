FREEING THE WHALES

WILL SEAWORLD EVER FREE THE WHALES?


APRIL 25, 2016 BY CHRISTINA BILBO


For too long SeaWorld has been holding killer whales hostage in captivity, with the lie that they are doing it with the whales' best interest in mind. But whales don't belong in bodies of water the size of a backyard family pool. Whales belong in the ocean. Removing them from their environment is detrimental not only on the physical well-being of the orca whales but on the mental wellness of them as well.

It is only until recently that SeaWorld has attracted so much negative attention, with the introduction of the 2013 movie "Blackfish". The movie opened the eyes of many individuals and showed them what really goes on in the shallow depths of the SeaWorld pools. The documentary film focuses on the poor treatment of the orca whales at SeaWorld facilities and how the workers handle the mammals.


"FREE, FREE, SET THEM FREE"


There have been many protests and petitions before and since the movie's release, and surprisingly, changes have been made. The whales are finally getting a breath of relief now that the california coastal commission has ruled that SeaWorld can no longer breed any of their captive whales. No longer will the whales face forced breeding with their brothers and sisters and be seperated from their children and parents. The Coastal Commission also approved a $100 million dollar plan to double the size of the killer whale containment habitat, allowing the behemoths to move more freely and explore their surroundings.
